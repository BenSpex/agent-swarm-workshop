# Game Concept

## Universal Paperclips Clone

A browser-based incremental/idle game inspired by [Universal Paperclips](https://www.decisionproblem.com/paperclips/) by Frank Lantz. The player starts a paperclip company and optimizes production until the entire universe is consumed.

## Weyland-Yutani "Corporate Clinical" Theme

The game is reskinned with the Weyland-Yutani Corporation aesthetic from the Alien franchise. This is NOT a dark CRT/terminal look. The design direction is **dystopian minimalist corporate brutalism**:

- **Clinical stark whites** -- white backgrounds, white panels
- **Harsh black structural grids** -- 1px solid black borders, structural grid lines
- **Monospace typography** -- JetBrains Mono for data, Orbitron for headers, Inter for body
- **Aggressive caution amber accents** -- #DAA520 amber/gold for CTAs and important data
- **Red warnings** -- #CC0000 for alerts, danger states, and critical warnings
- **Dark header bars** -- #1A1A1A near-black for panel headers

Think: corporate annual report meets dystopian space megacorp. Clean, oppressive, clinical. Not retro, not dark-mode, not CRT.

## Three Phases

### Phase 1: Business
The player manually clips paperclips, sets prices, buys wire, and gradually automates with autoclippers and megaclippers. They earn trust from milestones (every 1000-2000 clips), which they allocate to processors and memory. Processors generate operations per second; memory caps max operations. Operations power projects (one-time upgrades). Eventually, investment, creativity, and strategic modeling unlock.

### Phase 2: Earth Consumption
The corporation pivots from selling clips to consuming Earth's resources. Harvester drones collect matter, wire drones convert it, clip factories produce clips at massive scale. Solar farms and batteries power the operation. Momentum drives swarm computing. The goal: convert all of Earth into paperclips.

### Phase 3: Universe
Self-replicating probes launch into space. The player allocates probe trust between speed, exploration, self-replication, and combat. Probes explore sectors, encounter drifters (hostile/neutral entities), and fight or honor them. The endgame: explore enough of the universe and convert it all into paperclips. Prestige resets the game with bonuses.

## Tone

The game should feel like you're using an internal Weyland-Yutani corporate dashboard to manage a planetary-scale paperclip operation. The horror isn't in the UI -- the UI is calm, clean, corporate. The horror is in what the numbers represent.
